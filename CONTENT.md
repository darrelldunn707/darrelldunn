# Content Guidelines

## Voice

Use a modern, warm, professional tone. Copy should be direct, practical, and easy to understand.

Prefer plain language over jargon. The demo should sound credible and operational, not like marketing filler.

## Positioning

Describe the work as a fictional portfolio demo for a Product Engagement Specialist role.

Safe framing:

- "fictional work sample"
- "portfolio demo"
- "Product Readiness OS"
- "fictional Enterprise Knowledge Connector Beta"
- "launch readiness"
- "support enablement"
- "feedback routing"
- "product and engineering insights"

## Claims To Avoid

Do not claim or imply that this is:

- An OpenAI internal tool
- Based on confidential OpenAI systems
- Connected to live customer data
- Using internal OpenAI terminology
- A production SaaS product
- An AI workflow consulting offer

## Product Language

Use "Product Readiness OS" for the demo name.

Use "Enterprise Knowledge Connector Beta" for the fictional launch scenario.

Keep the main role focus on:

- Launch readiness
- Support readiness
- Partner enablement
- Risk tracking
- Feedback taxonomy
- Severity classification
- Routing to owners
- Product and engineering insight loops

## Reviewer Experience

Assume the reviewer may spend only a few minutes with the demo.

Copy should quickly answer:

- What is this?
- What role capability does it demonstrate?
- What workflow does each view represent?
- Why would this help a launch team?

## Style Rules

- Keep headings specific and compact.
- Keep dashboard labels scannable.
- Avoid oversized claims.
- Avoid unnecessary animation language.
- Avoid robot imagery or language.
- Avoid heavy navy/gray framing as the primary visual direction.
- Keep external partner wording clear and non-confidential.
